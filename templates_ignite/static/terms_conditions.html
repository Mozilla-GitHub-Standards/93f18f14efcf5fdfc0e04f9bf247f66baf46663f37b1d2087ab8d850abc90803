Overview:

Mozilla Foundation (“Mozilla” or “Sponsor”) is a global non-profit organization with a mission to promote openness, innovation and opportunity on the Web. In cooperation with the United States National Science Foundation (“NSF”), Mozilla presents this IDEAS Challenge (the “Ideas Challenge”) seeking ideas to enhance or improve the public’s understanding of how web apps running on next-generation networks could improve people’s everyday lives, particularly in the areas of: Health, Transportation, Clean Energy, Learning, Public Safety, and Advanced Manufacturing (the “Priority Areas”). Ideas accepted in the Challenge will be published to help inspire invention and development to benefit the Web community, including inspiration for projects in the separate DEVELOPMENT Challenge that will be conducted after this Ideas Challenge is completed.

When to Submit Ideas:

The Ideas Challenge begins on Tuesday, May 1st 2012 at 12:00:01 am Pacific Time (“PT”), and all submissions must be received by Friday June 22nd, 2012 at 11:59:59 pm PT (“Ideas Challenge Term”). You are responsible for knowing the time zone and date in your location. It is recommended not to wait until the last moment to submit an idea. Proof of submission is not proof of receipt. Sponsor’s database computer is the official timekeeping device for the Ideas Challenge.

Submission Form Requirements:

All Ideas Challenge participants must clearly and concisely complete all nine (9) sections of the idea-submission form:

  1. Name: What’s your idea called?
  2. The problem: What problem, need or challenge does your idea address? Why should people care? (250 words or less.)
  3. The solution: Describe your proposed solution. How does it address the problem? (400 words or less.)
  4. What focus area(s) does it address? Select one from the list on the entry page.
  5. How will your idea benefit the public? How will it make people’s lives better, or create positive social value? ( 200 words or less)
  6. Who will it help? What groups will it serve? At what scale? (200 words or less)
  7. How does your idea take advantage of next-generation networks? What existing technological limitations are holding your idea back? Does some version of your idea already exist? If so, how does your idea improve on it? What challenges do you anticipate in building it? (400 words or less)
  8. Anything else you’d like to add? Feel free to include links to visual assets, napkin sketches, video, or anything else that might help flesh out your idea.
  9. Are you interested in making your idea a reality? Do you want to try and build it? If so, do you need help? (For example, guidance, mentorship, learning materials, more team members . . . This won’t have an impact on how your idea is judged -- we’re just curious.) (100 words or less)

Submissions may not contain advertisements, commercial endorsements, or any material that is indecent, obscene, hateful, discriminatory, defamatory, slanderous, libellous, unlawful or otherwise inappropriate as determined by Sponsor in its absolute discretion. Any inappropriate Submissions or material may be disqualified by Sponsor in its absolute discretion.

Evaluation of Submissions:

Evaluation of Submissions will occur in two (2) rounds. The decisions of the judges will be final in all instances.

Round One - Submissions will be screened for basic compliance with the entry requirements and procedures, including but not limited to (a) The idea is in a Priority Area, and (b) The idea leverages the Global Environment for Network Innovations (GENI). Submissions meeting those requirements will be eligible to become published on the Ideas Challenge Website for public viewing and comment. No guarantee is made that any particular Submission will or will not be published or as to the length of time between finalization of the Submission and publication. Any ineligible Submission will be disqualified and not published. Visitors to the Ideas Challenge Website may “thumbs up” or comment on any of the published Submissions until the conclusion of the Ideas Challenge Term.

Round Two - On or about June 22nd, 2012, round two of judging will begin, and will continue until approximately July 9th, 2012, after which time the top Submissions will be announced publicly. Judging in Round Two will be by a review board of approximately twenty (20) individuals chosen by Mozilla. Judging will be based on the following criteria:

  • Impact: To what degree could this idea provide significant public benefit i.e. improve people’s lives? (35%)
  • Story: Is the potential benefit articulated in a clear and compelling way that will excite people? (ensure it avoids controversial public benefits) (25%)
  • Innovation: To what degree is this idea innovative? (25%)
  • GENI: To what degree does this idea leverage GENI? (10%)
  • Priority Area: To what degree could this idea add value in terms of serving an area of national priority? (5%)
